Objectives are being developed using Objectives Key Results (OKRs) aligned to our four BETTER pillars. This means each objective describes what you want to achieve (the Objective) and how you'll know you've achieved it (the Key Result), each mapped to a BETTER pillar: BETTER Technology, BETTER Efficiencies, BETTER Delivery or BETTER Company.

You are expected to create 5 objectives this year. Although objectives are set at the start of the year, they can be updated and added to at any time. You may not have them fully refined right now - especially in terms of measurement, but that shouldn't stop you from starting. Get them in and refine as we go.
If you haven't yet seen the training on the new approach and how to build effective objectives, visit the Capita Academy page here.

Why engagement is a core objective this year
When you work in a strategic role like ours, a core part of the job is bringing together different viewpoints and ideas from across industry, synthesising and evaluating them, and concluding on what's right for Capita. Without active industry engagement - whether that's contributing to working groups, attending conferences, publishing, or simply having regular conversations with peers and expert bodies - we risk drifting from what the wider industry is doing and the direction it's heading. As the strategy team, we are the captain of the ship, responsible for navigating industry challenges, staying aligned with expert bodies, and making sure Capita's security direction is informed by the best thinking available, not just our own.
With that in mind, this year's team objectives are built around three pillars of development and engagement. At least three of your five objectives should reflect the following:

1. Your professional development and growth (supports BETTER Company)
As a strategic business capability, everyone is expected to have completed at least one foundational AI training course from Capita's Academy by Q3/26. If you've already completed an AI foundations course, choose another AI-related module. In addition, you should agree a continuing professional development (CPD) plan this year, aligned to your role. CPD isn't just about courses - consider mentoring, coaching, developing or applying soft skills such as research, writing and presenting.

2. Internal active engagement (supports BETTER Technology, BETTER Delivery and BETTER Efficiencies)
This is about being visibly present and contributing your expertise across the business. Examples include delivering lunch-and-learn sessions, supporting Tom with your specialist discipline in our engagement plan, contributing to awareness and education in team meetings outside of CISO, and actively engaging on Viva Engage to raise awareness, provide guidance or spark thought leadership internally. In addition, everyone should recognise at least 5 colleagues this year for their excellent application of security knowledge and skills using our Celebrate! platform.

3. External active engagement (supports BETTER Technology, BETTER Delivery and BETTER Efficiencies)
This is about being visibly present and contributing your expertise outside the business.
For Band C roles, this could include gaining membership and accreditation with CIISec and actively engaging with their events and activities this year, as well as starting to build connections with institutional bodies that give you exposure to the breadth of your discipline and help you develop depth.
For Band D roles, the expectation is higher - actively engaging in thought leadership (not just one-way consumption, but genuine discussion and participation), joining specialist groups, contributing to publications, and building a wider industry presence as a professional. Critically, this isn't just about personal profile - it's about bringing that learning and thinking back into Capita to improve what we do.

Defining what good and outstanding looks like
Another historic challenge I want to address this year is how we measure strong versus outstanding performance. Being clear upfront gives both you and your line manager an agreed benchmark for what outstanding looks like, which makes end-of-year reviews fairer and more transparent. Without it, assessing whether someone has truly gone above and beyond becomes subjective - and that's demotivating when you've worked hard all year but can't clearly evidence the impact. So when writing your objectives, be explicit about what "achieve" and "exceed" look like for each one as part of the "Key Result".
